Scan Guide

Better photo in. Better baseline out.

This page exists so scan quality does not get buried inside the analysis screen. Keep the image clean, the face neutral, and the framing honest.

Run Analysis Back Home
01

Neutral

No smile, no jaw clench, no brows raised. Expression noise changes proportions and lowers the value of the baseline.

02

Centered

Keep the face straight to camera. Do not let the phone angle down or up if you want a usable read.

03

Visible

Show the forehead, upper hairline, and enough of the neck so the facial thirds and hairline step are not compromised.

Quick Checklist

Use this before every scan.

LightingUse flat, bright, forward light. Avoid side shadow, overhead spotlighting, and dark rooms.
Lens DistanceStand far enough back that the face is not distorted by extreme close-range perspective.
HairMove fringe or hair away from the forehead if it blocks the hairline and upper third.
ConsistencyIf you are comparing scans over time, try to keep the same framing and lighting every time.
Common Mistakes

What ruins the baseline.

SmilingChanges lower third, lip posture, and cheek shape.
Tight CropHurts the hairline step and can shift perceived proportions.
ShadowMakes symmetry and width reads less trustworthy.
Head TiltThrows off harmony and angle interpretation.
